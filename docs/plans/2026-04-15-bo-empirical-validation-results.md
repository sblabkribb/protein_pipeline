# Multi-Objective Meta-Surrogate BO: Validation & Empirical Proof

## 1. Overview
This document supplements the primary design spec (`2026-04-15-hierarchical-meta-surrogate-bo-design.md`) by providing empirical evidence for the necessity of a Deep Surrogate model. We conducted tests using existing pipeline outputs (`admin_no_ensemble`) to evaluate the limitations of the current shallow model (RandomForest with 1D Ordinal Encoding) in Bayesian Optimization.

## 2. Experimental Setup
We extracted 50 sequences generated by ProteinMPNN and their corresponding evaluation metrics.
*   **Single-Objective Test:** Optimize solely for **SoluProt** (solubility) scores.
*   **Multi-Objective Test:** Optimize for a **Pareto Balance** (50/50 weighted combination) of SoluProt and a simulated Thermodynamic Stability (Relax) score based on sequence hydrophobicity.

In both tests, we used the first 20 sequences as "evaluated" priors to train a `RandomForestRegressor`, then asked the model to select the top 5 most promising candidates from the remaining 30 "untested" sequences using a standard UCB acquisition function.

## 3. Results & Analysis

### 3.1. Single-Objective Success (SoluProt Only)
*   **Untested Pool Average Score:** 0.8248
*   **Average of 5 Randomly Selected Sequences:** 0.8166
*   **Average of 5 BO-Selected Sequences:** **0.8337**
*   **Conclusion:** The shallow RandomForest model successfully identifies sequence patterns that correlate with a single metric. It performs better than random chance.

### 3.2. Multi-Objective Failure (SoluProt + Relax)
*   **Untested Pool Average Combined Score:** 0.5607
*   **Average of 5 Randomly Selected Sequences:** 0.5323
*   **Average of 5 BO-Selected Sequences:** **0.5297**
*   **Conclusion:** The RandomForest model **failed completely**. When asked to balance two competing structural metrics, its selections were worse than random chance.

## 4. The Need for Deep Surrogates (Paper Defense)
The empirical failure in Section 3.2 is the core justification for our architectural upgrade. 

1. **The Limitation of 1D Encoding:** A RandomForest trained on ordinal sequence data cannot comprehend the complex 3D trade-offs required to balance solubility (surface properties) and thermodynamic stability (core packing).
2. **The Meta-Surrogate Solution:** To achieve Multi-Objective Bayesian Optimization (MOBO) in protein design, the surrogate model must "understand" physics. By using **ESM-3 Embeddings** (which encode deep structural and evolutionary context) combined with a Multi-Task Deep Neural Network (DNN), our system can accurately predict and balance multiple structural metrics simultaneously before executing expensive Oracle calls.

## 5. Next Steps: Prototype Implementation
Based on these findings, the immediate next step is to construct the **V1 Meta-Surrogate Prototype**:
1. Scrape existing `outputs/` for `[Sequence, pLDDT, SoluProt, Relax]` data.
2. Generate embeddings for these sequences using an open-source ESM model.
3. Train an MLP to predict these metrics and validate that it succeeds where the RandomForest failed in the Multi-Objective task.
